The Dracorex

About

Fun Fact

The Dracorex is also called the Dracorex Hogwarsia8, which means dragon king of Hogwarts. Although the Dracorex Hogwartsia is an interesting name, it is not a new species but a juvenile of Pachycepalorsaurus15.

Size

They were about 15 to 21 feet long as an adult, which is about the length of 2 cars. They reached a height of 4 feet. An adult Dracorex also weighed about 1000 pounds6.

Time Period

They live in the Maastrichtian age of the Late Cretaceous. This was about 72 to 66 million years ago10. This was the last age od Cretaceous period before the new Paleogene period.

Diet

The Pachycephalosaurus was a herbivore. Their diet consisted of fruits, leaves and seeds17. Some scientists believed that the Pachycephalosaurus might have been an omnivore because of their sharp teeth which resemble carnivore teeth.

Predators

The Tyrannosaur16 was one of the Pachycephalosaurus predators. The Tyrannosaur was 40 feet long and 12 feet tall. The Pachycephalosaurus could not do anything against the Tyrannosaur. Another predator that they had to deal with was the velociraptor. They were 5.5 feet long and 1.6 feet tall. The Pachycephalosaurus lived in a herd whiched helped with defending themselves against Velociraptors.

Identification

They could easily be identified by their unique bone dome on their head. They are also bipedal16, which means that they walk on two feet. They were about 15 to 21 feet long as an adult and they reached a height of 4 feet.

Fossil Record

The fossils were found in the layer of upper Creteceous rock14. The layer was mostly made out of sandstones and siltstone. There was also conglomerate, shale, mudstone, and porcelanite.

Fossil Description

Their fossils were easily recognizable because of their unique features on their heads. They had a bone dome on their head which was unique to their species. Their skull was 30 times thicker than a human skull13. They also had small triangular teeth, which scientists suspected that they might have eaten meat. Nevertheless they were herbivores.

Who Discovered It and When

The fossil of Pachycephalosaurus was discovered by Loren Colman and Robert Bakker11. They discovered the fossil in 2004. The fossils were found in Hell Creek Formation, South Dakota.

Habitat

Pachycephalosaurus lived in forests, grasslands and plains. The habitat had delta river, shrubs, woodlands, ferns and ponds. The ecosystem was shared with invertebrates, birds, reptiles, and amphibians. This habitat was great for the Pachycephalosaurus because it was filled with luscious vegetation which they ate. Their predators also inhabited the same environment like the tyrannosaurus rex17, velociraptor, carnotaurus, and allosaurus.

Distribution

The Pachycepalorsaurus used to roam around what is now North America. To be more precise they lived in the province of Alberta7, Canada and in the North of the West and Midwest states of the United States. The figure below shows an image of Canada and the United States.

Communication

They communicated vocally and visually. They would usually grunt or show their postures to communicate5. Sometimes they get physical to communicate with each other. This usually happens during mating season.

Behavior and Adaptation

They live in a herd to protect the juvenile Pachycephalosaurus against predators. When a predator threatens a juvenile Pachycephalosaurus they use their dome head to head butt the predators. They are usually passive creatures but they show they become agressive when mating season comes or to show dominance to become herd leader12.

Breeding Behavior

The Pachycephalosaurus is similar to some animals like Bighorn sheep. They used their bone dome head to show superiority by head butting another one of their species in the flank7. This only happens during mating season for male to court mates.

Life Cycle

The Pachycephalosaurus starts off as an egg. Then they break out of their egg and become a hatchling. After a while they grow up to be a juvenile. As they grow older their skull gets thicker. When they become adults their skull will reach a thickness of about 10 inches9 and the cycle repeats.
