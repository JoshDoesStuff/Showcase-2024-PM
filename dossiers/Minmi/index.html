Introduction

Minmi, also called by its scientific name Minmi paravertebra, a prehistoric dinosaur that roamed the Earth approximately 110 million years ago during the Early Cretaceous period. Discovered by Dr. Alan Bartholomai and a local in Queensland in 1964. Minmi paravertebra was a small dinosaur for that time, with an estimated length of 2 to 3 meters and a height of less than 1 meter, as you can see in the right a figure of it. This dinosaur was found in marine rocks. It was in 1980 that Raph Molnar dictate the name we recognize today. We know that Minmi paravertebra lived in the Early Cretaceous era with their contemporaries Muttaburrasaurus and Leaellynasaura, that was about 110 million years ago. 1 & 2

Habitat

Minmi lived in ancient Australia, a land that existed within the supercontinent of Gondwana during the Early Cretaceous era, approximately 110 million years ago. The exact place where MInmi lived was characterized by biodiversity. The vegetation goes from ferns, cycads, conifers, and a lot more of other ancient plants. These flora served as the primary way of feeding to the Minmi paravertebra. The landscape was lowland, with a large network of rivers and lakes. A good comparative of this habitat is today's rainforest, is similar in vegetation and amount of water. 3 & 4

Feeding and Diet

Minmi was a herbivore, Minmi's menu consisted in a diverse type of plants, some examples are ferns, cycads, and other soft-leaved prehistoric plants. It also fed on seeds and fruits of flowers. The Minmi's teeth were in charge of processing the food, they were efficient in their work since there was no possible rock or any indication that they had failed in the process of digested food. 3 & 6

How to identify it

Minmi paravertebra was recognizable by its distinctive body armor, consisting of bony plates or scutes intricately embedded in its skin, similar to a turtle shell. Minmis height is less than 1 meter, a small herbivorous dinosaur characterized by a low body profile. Minmi walked on four short legs and had a small head and a medium tail. 2 , 3 , 6 & 7

Behaviors

Minmi paravertebra has an armed shell that is adapted to protect itself from predators. Although it is not as strong as other dinosaurs, it is possible to survive because it used other ways to protect itself, such as hiding in bushes due to its short stature and camouflage. 5 , 7 & 8

Fun Fact

By the Late Cretaceous, ankylosaurus from North America and Asia were much larger and better armed than Minmi. They had developed a large bony club at the end of their tail, probably to protect them from attack by tyrannosaurs. From: Western Australian Museum 3

Communication Life History Cycle Breeding
The communication of this dinosaur remains a mystery due to the limited fossil records. But it can be deduced through similarities with other dinosaurs, it probably uses a combination of vocalization and body language to communicate, either for messages of protection or to court a mate. 5 , 7 & 8 It begins with the hatching of the egg, and continues with the growing of the dinosaur, then adulthood and finally reproduction. It is known that the Minmi belongs to the group of oviparous animals. But the exactness of its cycle remains a matter of research. 5 , 7 & 8 Minmi is oviparous which means that when you reproduce it lays eggs. Scientists believe that the Minmi had protective behavior towards its eggs and off springs but there is a lack of evidence to confirm this. 5 , 7 & 8

Predators

Potential predators of Minmi included larger theropod dinosaurs like Australovenator and Kakuru kujani. Minmi had its body armor that it used to protect from them. 6

Australovenator

Theropod dinosaur, found in Australia, and lived at creation. Carnivore dinosaur that had large claws and arms that it used to attack its prey. 9

Kakuru kujani

Karuku dinosaur from the Cretaceous period also found in Australia in the early 1970s. This dinosaur is characterized by its strong claws on its feet that it uses to attack its prey. 10
