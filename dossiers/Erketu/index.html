Dino Dossier, Erketu

Erketu

The Erketu is a long neck sauropod from the Late Cretaceous period,
which was about 96 to 89 million years ago, and discovered in Mongolia at the Bor Guvé excavation site.
The Erketu is about 15 meters tall, which is around 49 feet tall and lives in floodplains,
coastal terrains or any wet and coastal habitats.

Biology

Erketu dinosaurs roamed around Asia during the Late Creaceous period, with lack of information
to tell of its behaviors. It is estimated that they were herbivores with a diet that includes:

  • Plants.
  • Fruits.
  • Small Mammals.
  • Lizards.
  • Amphibia.

The Erketus used a way of communication by using visual, tactile, and auditory ways to communicate to
each other. The fossils suggest the long neck the Erketu has, the hindlimb material shows the body proportions
which is a result of a greatly elongated vertebrae and also had bifurcate anterior cervical neural spines.
They have predators of Gorgosaurus, which is a relative of the Tyrannosaurus Rex.
