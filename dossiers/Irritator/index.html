Size Range

The Irritator Challengeri usually sized between 6 and 8 meters in its length. This makes it one of the smallest known spinosaurids. Spinosaurids themselves are part of the largest land-dwelling animals in Earth’s history. [1] [1]

Habitat

Lagoons surrounded with dry regions. In these aquatic regions, they would hunt and forage, eating fish and other types of dinosaurs. Based on skull shape, it was adapted to hunting aquatically from its ability to see above the waterline while submerged. [2]

Identification

Evidence of this terrible lizard spawns from the distinct shape of its fossil skull, the jaw is made for fast biting motions similar to that of a pelican. Elongated snout. Conical teeth. Specifically, it is a spinosaurid based on the skull’s appearance. [4]

Who Discovered it and When

The precise discovery site is unknown, but the famous skull was found somewhere in Santana do Cariri by fossil dealers who sold it to the State Museum of Natural History. In 1996, the skull was identified with how we view it today by paleontologists Martill, Cruikshank, Frey, Small and Clarke in Brazil. [1]

Time Period + contemporaries

Early Cretaceous period, in the Albian stage over 100 million years ago. Caudipteryx, Fukuiraptor, Gastonia, and types of pterosaurs lived in the world at this time. [2]

Feeding and Diet

The irritator was a generalist when it came to what types of animals were part of their diet. Based on the mouth shape and location, they primarily ate fish, turtles and smaller dinosaurs. It’s believed that they acted crocodilian in nature. [2]

Behaviors and Adaptations

Reliant on sense of smell, adapted to hunt semi-aquatically. Their teeth are not serrated, making them function for grabbing and holding prey, which differ from other carnivores. The skull gives evidence of neck musculature that would help the irritator move its head quickly and pull its jaw against water. [2]

Communication

The Irritators most likely used special gestures and distinct sounds to communicate to one another. There is no concrete evidence of how they sounded or acted physically, but it can be assumed that they had systems and inner workings to interact with their own.

Life History Cycle

The Irritator would live life surviving, hunting and eating other animals, being mainly a piscivore. Hunting fish in lagoons of water around dry territories. [3]

Predators

Though it was part of the spinosaurid family, it also had its share of predators. Larger carnivores, types of pterosaurs, generally any larger dinosaurs that were also carnivores provided a threat to these animals.

Fossil Description/record

The discovered fossil of this specimen mostly includes the skull of the irritator, slightly damaged by fossil collectors and making issues with identification of the species. The irritator’s teeth are a physical component that led paleontologists to believing that it was a piscivore. Incomplete skull of the Irritator Challengeri, accompanied with other additional finds.
