The Triceratops

Scale

The average size of the Triceratops was around 9 meters long. Their head made up around one third of their body, its range was about 10 meters long.

Habitat

The Triceratops tended to live in dry, forested areas and plains with plenty of vegetation. Due to them being herbivores, they need plants to survive instead of meat like carnivores. Their ecosystems were heavily forested, and much more different than they are in the present day. To find out about the climate and environment in a certain period of time, scientists use the remaining stone around the fossils to determine what it was like in the prehistoric times.

Distribution

Triceratops fossils are mostly found in North America, in the United States. Fossils were located in Colorado, Montana, Wyoming, South Dakota, and a few were in Canada. In fact, the first recorded fossil was found near Denver, Colorado, meaning that back in the late Cretaceous period North America was filled with vegetation and forests, due to the lack of human interference. There is no information on if triceratops fossils are somewhere other than the United States, the act of fossilization, or an animal becoming preserved in a fossilized form, occurs at specialized sites where remnants of bones are to be encased in the surrounding rocks.

Identification

The Triceratops, whose Latin name translates to “three-horned face,” is one of the most recognized of every dinosaur, due to its familiar frill and horns. Along their frill, they possessed a quantity of small spikes that are called epoccipitals, and usually had around 19-26 of them. The Triceratops had a beak-like mouth that helped with tough vegetation and an abnormally large head that was around 10 feet long, making it one of the largest animals to ever roam the Earth.

Feeding and Diet

Triceratops were herbivores, feeding on low vegetation. Scientists can determine this by taking a closer look at their teeth. Since their teeth are more flattened, it made it easier to grind plant matter, therefore showing that they had a diet of plants in the surrounding area. The Triceratops most likely feasted on ferns, cycads and palms due to these plants being in abundance in the heavily forested areas.

Behaviors and Adaptations

Triceratops most likely battled amongst themselves, as shown by injuries that were caused by other Triceratops. Puncture marks on frills suggested that male triceratops used their horns when fighting each other, possibly to impress females. Frills with holes in them were discovered and showed signs of healing, the fighting between Triceratops were most likely due to the battle over territory or breeding.

Communication

Triceratops most likely traveled alone most of their lives, rather than traveling in herds like other animals. Remains of Triceratops were often found spread out, they were rarely found in groups of 3 or more individual triceratops. Even though they may have traveled alone, they still fought amongst themselves over territory and mates.

Life History Cycle

They usually grew up to 8 to 9 meters long and weighed around 5 to 9 metric tons. The largest skull of a Triceratops was estimated to have been 2.5 meters, or 8.2 feet, in length, making up around one third of the length of the entire body. The Triceratops was one of the largest dinosaurs in body mass.

Breeding Behaviors

Male Triceratops left their family groups during reproductive season to find mates. They may have herded male rivals away from potential mates or competed against other males. Their frill might have been lined with blood vessels that help change the color of it to communicate danger, anger, or receptiveness to mate.

Predators

It is believed that the Tyrannosaurus preyed upon triceratops, due to them living in the same time period. A Triceratops fossil was found with a bitten off horn with bite marks that match a Tyrannosaurus rex. It is most likely that their horns helped to fend off attacks from the Tyrannosaurus.

Fossil Description

Triceratops hind limbs were larger than their forelimbs, but overall both sets were stout. The skull of the Triceratops had frill on the back of it and had a beak that was in front of the teeth. The 3 horns on the skull of the Triceratops most likely acted as defensive weapons to protect themselves from danger.
