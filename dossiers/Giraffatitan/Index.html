Giraffatitan Dossier

Classification

Genus Giraffatitan
Species Girafatitan Brancai
Family Brachiosauridae
Suborder Sauropodomorpha
Order Saurischia
Phylum Chordata
Kingdom Animalia

Size

An adult Giraffatitan can weigh up to about
40 metric tons and be up to 23 meters tall,
about 72 ft. The size of the dinosaur was based
on the specimen HMN SII which was a subadult.

Location & Habitat

The Giraffatitans were
located in modern day
Tanzania, Africa. They also
lived in areas such as:

  • lagoon-like marine environments
  • tidal flats
  • low coastal environments
  • vegetated inland environments

Fossil Record & Identification

The Giraffatitan had a similar body to a giraffe with longer legs
and a much longer neck. “ The skull had a tall arch anterior to the eyes,
consisting of the bony nares, a number of other openings, and ‘spatulate’ teeth,
like chisels.” The first toe on the front feet as well as the first three toes
on the back feet had claws. There are very few recorded fossils of
the Giraffatitan. There are five partial skeletons, three skulls, and many
fragments of skull material, bones, vertebrae, and teeth.

Giraffatitan Discovery

The Giraffatitan was discovered and named in 1914 by
Werner Janensch at a German mining site in Tanzania
where several specimens were recovered.

Feeding & Adaptations

The Giraffatitan were herbivores, it is estimated that they
needed to eat more than 400 lbs of food per day to sustain
themselves. They were able to reach higher trees than other
plant eating dinosaurs thanks to their longer neck and legs.
Its front legs were longer than its hind legs
so that it could reach even higher altitudes.
