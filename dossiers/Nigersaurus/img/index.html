Nigersaurus

Classification

  • Genus: Nigersaurus
  • Species: Taqueti
  • Family: Rebbachisauridae
  • Suborder: Sauropodomorpha
  • Order: Sauropoda
  • Phylum: Chordata
  • Kingdom: Animalia

Image

The Nigersaurus dinosaurs resided in the Early Cretaceous period, about 145 million years ago. Located in the West African country of Niger, this type of dinosaur has some niche aspects that make it unique to the rest. They roam in African plains and woodlands feeding on low-lying plants as their herbivorous diet. They act like other herbivorous sauropods and they lay eggs for more to be born. Their physical attributes are their long necks, long tail, having a lot of small teeth, thin bones, and their fascinating face. Their jaws are weak, having more width than their entire skull, it may be the reason for their diet. It was assumed that their long necks were hard to position. Their predators are spinosaurids, carcharodontosaurus, and abelisaurids but the likely cause of their extinction was their low diet. French paleontologist Philippe Taquet discovered the Nigersaurus fossil remains in 1976 but little bones were found. It was considered an unknown species until 1997 and 2000s, where more fossils were discovered. The name Nigersaurus, was made by Professor Paul Sereno.
