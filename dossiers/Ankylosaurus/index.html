Identification

The Ankylosaurus was a prehistoric tank — an armored dinosaur with a club-like protrusion at the end of its tail, weighing over 3000kg and measuring over 6m in length.[1]The top of the dinosaur was covered with thick armor made up of large knobs and oval plates of bone called osteoderms or scutes, giving the Ankylosaurus a very rugged appearance; osteoderms are also present on crocodiles and armadillos. These plates, probably covered in skin and keratin, were aligned in horizontal rows down the neck, back, and hips of the dinosaur, also covering the end of the dinosaur's tail to create a club. Smaller plates of armor between larger plates as well as possibly having plates on its limbs and tail further fortified the Ankylosaurus. Offering additional protection were 2 rows of spikes down the body, horns projecting back and to the side, plates covering the animal’s eyes, and a flat body low to the ground. The animal's large skull was equipped with a narrow beak and leaf-shaped teeth, and its hind limbs were slightly longer than those in the front. These characteristics created the unique appearance of the Ankylosaurus, easily recognizable by its armored body and tail club.[2]

Habitat & Distribution

During the Cretaceous Period, North America was divided by the Western Interior Seaway, with the island continent of Laramdia to the west and the landmass of Appalachia to the east. The Ankylosaurus inhabited Laramdia, which became present-day western North America.[3] The Ankylosaurus’ range stretched across western North America, with fossils found between Alberta and Wyoming. The armored dinosaur lived in a terrestrial environment. It resided in the woodlands, plains, and grasslands that received the abundant rainfall necessary to sustain its diet of greens as a herbivore.[1] Ankylosauruses may have lived in environments away from rivers and swamps conducive to fossilization, which could possibly explain why their fossils are so rare.[2]

Diet

The Ankylosaurus was a herbivore. Because of its stature, it grazed on low-lying plants — up to 60kg a day, which is as much as a large elephant.[4] Their diet likely included ferns, cycads, shrubs, leaves, and fruit within their reach. The Ankylosaurus was well adapted to its herbivorous lifestyle. Its triangular skull housed a narrow beak at the end which aided in stripping leaves from the plants it ate on a daily basis. The small leaf-shaped teeth of the dinosaur could not break apart large plants and it also had no grinding teeth. Its large belly, however, indicates that the Ankylosaurus had an elaborate, possibly fermentation-based stomach system to break down all of the unchewed plants it ate.[2]Furthermore, Ankylosaurus’ fossils seem to indicate that it had a large brain structure dedicated to smell, possibly allowing it to use its sense of smell when finding food.[5] Analysis of its skull also suggests that the dinosaur's nostrils were placed at the side of its head, which may have served an evolutionary purpose to its diet or sense of smell.[6]

Behaviours & Communication

Although Juvenile Ankylosaurus likely lived in congregation for collective security,[7] adult Ankylosauruses likely lived a slow-moving solitary lifestyle, only meeting with other members of their species to mate. Their heavy, tank-like body paired with their short limbs made for a less than ideal combination when it comes to long-distance walking in herds. Furthermore, an adult Ankylosaurus would be more than capable of defending itself against larger predators with its armored body and tail club.[8].When the Ankylosaurus did meet each other to mate, the use of their tail clubs in breeding is disputed by scientists. The formidable weapon may have been used to simply attract mates but could have also been used to fight for mating rights/territory in addition to its role of protection against predators.[2]How the Ankylosauruses communicated is also unknown, but its complex sinus and nasal cavities, among other things like lightening weight or housing a nasal gland, could have been used for vocalization.[9]

Life History Cycle

Like all dinosaurs, the Ankylosaurus began its life as an egg, but its growth and development from this point forward is largely unknown. However, studies of Ankylosaurs offer insight into the growth and development of Ankylosauruses. A mixture of woven and parallel fibered bone paired with overall poor development of blood vessels in organs or parts indicates growth rates slower than other dinosaurs in Ankylosaurs. Additionally, Ankylosaurs substitute large amounts of their primary tissue early in their development. This anomaly could be linked to late formation of bone in their body armor.[10]Peculiar finds like the ones mentioned complicate the process of predicting the growth patterns of these dinosaurs, but their estimated lifespans are about 70-80 years old.[11] Once fully grown, the Ankylosaurus measured 6m-6.5m in length and weighed between 3000kg-4000kg. The girth of its stomach was 1.5m, which was its widest part, while its hips cleared the ground by about 1.7m, which was the tallest portion of its body. An adult skull was wider than it was long, at 25 inches in length and 29 inches in width. Taken together, these features made the Ankylosaurus the largest known Ankylosaurid.[1]

Time Period & Interactions

The Ankylosaurus lived during the late Cretaceous Period (about 65 - 68 million years ago). The dinosaur shared its environment with 2 of the most well-known dinosaurs, the T-rex and the triceratops. The Ankylosaurus also shared its environment with two well-known herbivores: The Edmontosaurus and the Pachycephalosaurus.[12]Carnivorous dinosaurs, most notably the Tyrannosaurus and Deinonychus were known to be natural enemies of the Ankylosaurus.[13] However, it is possible that these dinosaurs would avoid hunting the Ankylosaurus, instead going for easier meals. The dinosaur's heavy armor, tail club, and its rotund, flat body acted as strong deterrents. Flipping the Ankylosaurus to expose its soft underbelly was likely the only way for predators to defeat the formidable dinosaur.

Fossils

A team of paleontologists led by Barnum Brown discovered the first fossils of the Ankylosaurus in the Hell Creek Formation of Montana in 1906. This find included ribs, vertebrae, the top of a skull, armor, and a shoulder girdle piece.[2] However, Osteoderms previously found by Brown in the Lance Formation of Wyoming during the year 1900 are believed to belong to the Ankylosaurus instead of belonging to the Dynamosaurus, with whom the osteoderms were first associated. No complete Ankylosaurus skeleton has ever been found, with only 5 major specimens discovered when excluding isolated bones, armor, and teeth. These 5 specimens, which include Brown's holotype, are the main resource for studies on the Ankylosaurus as they each contain rare and important parts of its skeleton.[14] The dinosaur had fused bones in its skull and other parts of its body to provide extra strength, which is why its name means “fused lizard” in Greek. Like the osteoderms of all ankylosaurs, the Ankylosaurus’ osteoderms were made up of a thin compact bone on top of a thicker inner bone that is spongy and porous. The dinosaur's tail had vertebrae woven together, forming a stiff rod at the base of its characteristic tail club. Examination of the Ankylosaurus fossils also reveals that the neck was the most heavily armored region on the dinosaur.[15][2]
