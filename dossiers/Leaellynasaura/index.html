The Leaellynasaura Amicagraphica, a common dinosaur from the Cretaceous Period. It’s small in size, about 1 to 2 meters, and eats vegetation as a food source. Inhabited Australia, and had sharp teeth and claws, but a rather weird head as the seed-shaped beak sprouted from its snout, and was feathery or furry like a bird [1].

Fossil and Identification

With sharp claws and teeth, the Leaellynasaura was a beaked dinosaur with a long, big and slender tail. The beak connected to the snout, which made it look like lips and it had an arched neck. Its legs were long, great for running and short arms that it kept close to its body. A three toed dinosaur with another at its heel. Not much is known about the color of the dinosaur, but one thing for sure is that it had some sort of feathers or fur, good for protecting against pests, insects, and a fatal wound. The fossil had a toothed maxilla, skull roof, large eye sockets, and an endocast on the brain. An endocast is a hollow part of the brain [1].

Behavior and Diet

A very common cretaceous dinosaur that roamed the lower latitudes of earth. They created burrows and possibly hibernated during winter. In order to hibernate, they must store food. The Leaellynasaura ate edible and low growing plants, such as horse horsetails and ferns. Food was necessary for them during the winter, as there would be no more plants that could survive the winters. Which is why they communicated using their voice and body language. To either warn the others or tell them that food or a water hole was near [1].
