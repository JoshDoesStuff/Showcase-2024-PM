Size

The Tyrannosaurus rex is one of the most well known dinosaurs in the world. Originating from the United States of America, this 40 feet long and 12 feet high dinosaur has a rich history of life and the way it was discovered.

Habitat

The Tyrannosaurus rex originated in North America and doesn't have a fossil anywhere else. Fossils were discovered in the Southern side of the United States and in Canada as well.

Era

Tyrannosaurus rex lived in the Ceratourous era, so the tyrannosaurus is able to find meat really easily and able to live longer than most dinosaurs. The Tyrannosaurus rex also lived with other dinosaurs such as the Stokesosaurus, Ceratosaurus, Allosaurus, and Torvosaurus. It also allowed Tyrannosaurus rexes to find prey easily.

Identification

The Tyrannosaurus Rex is identified as a Bipedal Carnivore, meaning it eats meat while walking on two legs compared to other dinosaurs which uses all 4 legs to try and move.

Diet

Food was easy to find for Tyrannosaurus Rexes. Tyrannosaurus rex usually live in big forests near rivers. They chose rivers because they knew that there would be prey hanging around rivers to try and take a drink of water. The tyrannosaurus rex would then grab the prey out of mid-air to eat. Because of their substantial teeth, they often hunted for herbivorous dinosaurs. This made them have a population of around 1.7 billion Tyrannosaurus rexes during the Ceratourous era. With that many Tyrannosaurus rexes, they needed to learn how to properly live. 5

Lifestyle And Behavior

A normal lifestyle of a Tyrannosaurus Rex is a little bit more complicated than what most people know about these types of dinosaurs. Although they had a big size and sharp teeth, they had to deal with potential predators that are quick and able to attack them easily, even their own kind aren't friendly towards each other. As a result, they would often be put in packs so they were able to look out for each other incase of potential danger. The reason we know all of these things is because of a famous fossil hunter.

Discovery

The Tyrannosaurus Rex wasn't discovered until we hit the early 1900s when a man named Barnum Brown first discovered the fossil when Brown was leading his normal expedition. Having experience in the American Museum of Natural History, Brown decided to go to Hell Creek Formation in Montana, then he dug up what is believed to be the first documented evidence of the Tyrannosaurus Rex.4

Communication

One of the biggest things people like to assume about Tyrannosaurus rexes is that they are loud and often scream. Although this is partly true, most Tyrannosaurus Rex often communicate via tiny “coos” and mumbles.

Fossils!

The type of fossils that were found of the Tyrannosaurus Rex were sized like a 2 story building. It is notable for its size compared to other fossils of dinosaurs. As of 10/22/23, the only documented full findings were in the northwest of the United States and Canada. 4

Life History Cycle

Besides Tyrannosaurus Rexes living in packs and often getting into altercation with each other, they didn't live as long as other dinosaurs, often lasting about 28 years. They were often born in elongated eggs, having around 20 baby T-rexes. They often died to natural causes instead of dinosaurs because of their lack of predators that are threats to them.
