Khaan

indentification

Khaans were identified by their large beak and eyes as well as being land moving raptor like animals with small wings for arms.They closely resembled avian species possibly having bright feathers.Additionally they ran on two legs and would normally be found in packs.[2]Khans were about 43 (3½feet) inches tall.[5]

Feeding and Diet

The Khaan was believed to be an omnivore that would feed on small plants ,animals and rodents.One possible prey for the Khaan would have been the Catopsbaatar[7] which was a small rodent that existed at the same time during the late cretaceous.Moreover the Khaan possibly ate plants however it is unknown what plants the Khaan ate or if it even ate plants at all.[6]

Fossil Records

Khaan fossils were found mainly in the southern parts of Mongolia.There were very few Khaan fossils ever found with only two ever being discovered.They existed around 81-75 million years ago in the late Cretaceous era.They lived in a terrestrial habitat meaning they walked on land.[3]

Distribution/Habitat

Khaans were found in the sand dunes of Mongolia and mainly walked on land.Additionally they were known to be found in the Djadochta Formation which existed in the late cretaceous period.The Djadochta Formation was mainly made up of large deserts with little to no water and harsh predators sutch as the velociraptor.[3]

Cretaceous period

The Cretaceous period was about 81-75 million years ago and happened in the middle of the Mesozoic era.The Cretaceous period comes directly after the Jurassic period and is often remembered as the era when pangea began to split apart.It lasted nearly 80 million years making it the longest lasting geological period.It is often split between the early and late Cretaceous periods due to how long it was, the Khaan lived in the Late Cretaceous.[4]

Malcolm Mckenna

Malcolm Mckenna born in Pomona CA 1931.He was a paleontologist who would discover the Khaan. He was also known for founding Claremont Mckenna college.Additionaly he was the creator of Vertebrate paleontology. Malcolm Mckenna would pass away on March 3. 2008 in Boulder colorado.[8]

                   Behavior/Communicaion

Khaans were thought to have a very Sophisticated social structure with each having a different job.As a result of this Khaans were a very social species and typically stuck in packs to avoid predators.Khaans showed great care for their eggs and were known to create special nests for them to hatch in.Khaans where also believed to be egg thieves for a time though recent studies show this may be false.[1] 

                             Breeding

Khaans were known to lay eggs in nests.Guarding their young until they grew old enough to fend for themselves.They also possibly stole eggs from other dinosaurs raising them as their own.[1] 

                               life cycle

Khaans started as eggs and did not give live birth.After that they were juveniles being cared for by their parents.Then they became adults and had children of their own.Finally they became elders and lived out their lives until death.[1]
